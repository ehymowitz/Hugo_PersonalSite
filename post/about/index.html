About Me

Hi! Thanks for visiting my website! This is still VERY much a work in progress.

It is also where I get to write and post about things that I make and work on. So if you’re interested in learning about me this is the place for that. This part of the website is a little bit about me!

I’m originally from Seattle, Washington but I’ve been living in Montreal for the last 7 years (since 2012). Before I came here, I spent a gap year living in Israel. While studying engineering at McGill I developed an appreciation for all STEM related topics, and also realized my passion for playing music. I picked up the bass guitar and never really put it down.

I began playing music with a few friends from school in a pop/punk band called Free Hats (you can find a few of our songs on Spotify), and within a year I began playing with Aquarius Dreams - a band with which I’m still proud to be playing today! While playing with these bands, I got involved in the vibrant Montreal jam scene, a network of events wherein musicians meet up in a live setting and just play! They’re a great way to meet some great people and to try out new musical styles. I still frequently attend these jams, and am always thrilled to meet new musicians and play new music.

Nowadays, I’m living in Montreal working at a great startup a called Haply Robotics. At Haply we work in the field of haptics, robotics dealing with the recreation of touch. Our goal is to make this technology accessible to those who wish to use and learn with it. We work across a wide range of aspects of this technology, but the core belief is that using haptics, interaction with technology takes on a whole new positive dimension.

Aside from all this, I do a little bit of freelance work; teaching music, STEM topic tutoring, web design, and I’m beginning to get into business development as well. If you’d like examples of my work or want to know anything more about me, I’d love to hear from you! You can also check out the links on the side panel of this website to see any of my social media - including my GitHub where I have a few projects.
